Bio

I'm an first-year Computer Science PhD student at the University of Chicago advised with Professor Mina Lee. My research is in Human-AI interaction with a focus on understanding how technology can be used to deepen student learning by fostering instrinsic motivation and developing sustained resilience.

I am currently focused on two domains:

(1) how to promote information seeking behaviors in students when using LLMs

(2) understanding the social dynamics around when, where, from whom, students ask for help.

Before joining UChicago, I did my B.A. at Barnard College, double majoring in Computer Science and English (concentration in Creative Writing). I earned English Departmental Honors and the Theodore R. Bashkow Undergraduate Research Award in Computer Science. I was fortunate to be advised by Jhumpa Lahiri, for my Creative Writing Thesis. Throughout my undergraduate career, I did research with Professor Lydia Chilton on Human-AI co-writing tools to support science communication and with Professor Smaranda Muresan
